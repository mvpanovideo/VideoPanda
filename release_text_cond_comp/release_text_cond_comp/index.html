VideoPanda: OOD Text-conditional Comparison on VBench prompts (all_dimensions_longer)

Here we show qualitative comparisons from our text-conditional OOD evaluations using the full VBench suite of prompts (1700+ prompts) with great diversity and coverage of different dimensions and categories. We found that the base model we used (VideoLDM) can result in poor generalization to OOD prompts, which primarily stems from the weakness of the base model itself. So we implemented the VideoPanda method on top of a more powerful model, CogVideoX-2B which is a medium-sized transformer DiT. In particular, on the left column is the result from the model in our paper VideoPanda (VideoLDM + WEB360), the middle column is VideoPanda applied to the CogVideoX-2B model but trained with the exact same data VideoPanda (VideoLDM + WEB360) and the right column is the result from 360DVD using their standard implementation 360DVD (AnimateDiff + WEB360).

Most of the 360DVD (right column) have very distorted content in the sky and ground views (above or below +/-60 degree elevation). These are very visible when viewing the sky or ground views using the provided VR viewer.
Viewing results this way also highlights the strange motions in 360DVD including the ground morphing in a spiral direction (0) “In a charming Parisian café, a panda” or inanimate objects sliding in different directions (17) “A serene scene unfolds with a delicate porcelain teacup” when comparing VideoPanda (left column) vs 360DVD (rightmost column). With no alterations to our framework, the new model VideoPanda (CogVideoX-2B) (middle column) demonstrates large improvements in OOD generalization and video quality in both the text-conditional and video-conditional settings.
